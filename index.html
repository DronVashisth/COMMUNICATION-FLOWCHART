NON-VERBAL COMMUNICATION TYPES
Different ways to communicate non-verbally.
Body Language
Definition: Refers to physical behaviours, such as posture, gestures, and facial expressions, used to communicate non-verbally.
Paralinguistic
Definition: The vocal elements that accompany speech, which include tone, pitch, volume, and rate of speech.
Proxemics
Definition: The study of personal space and how physical distance between individuals affects communication.
Haptics
Definition: The use of touch in communication.
Appearance
Definition: The way individuals present themselves through clothing, grooming, and overall style.
Artefacts
Definition: Objects and personal belongings that individuals use to communicate identity or social status.
Kinesics
Definition: The study of body movements, gestures, and facial expressions as a form of communication.
Chronemics
Definition: The study of how time affects communication.
INTER PERSONAL COMUNICATION
Definition: Communication that occurs within an individual.
Main Points:
1.Involves self-talk, inner dialogue, and reflection.
2.Silent and internal process.
3.Helps in decision-making, processing thoughts, analyzing experiences, and managing emotions.
4.Influences attitudes, behaviors, and self-perception.
5.Examples: Reflecting on personal experiences, planning problem-solving approaches, rehearsing conversations.

INTERA PERSONAL COMUNICATION
Definition: Communication that occurs between two or more individuals, encompassing both verbal and non-verbal forms.
Main Points:
1.Fundamental for building relationships and resolving conflicts.
2.Can be formal or informal.
3.Includes verbal (spoken/written) and non-verbal (body language, facial expressions) elements.
4.Active listening, empathy, and feedback are key.
5.Examples: Conversations with friends, discussions with colleagues, counseling sessions.
GROUP COMUNICATION
Definition: Communication within a group involving three or more people.
Main Points:
1.Facilitates sharing information, decision-making, and problem-solving.
2.Can be structured or unstructured.
3.Involves group dynamics like roles, norms, and power structures.
4.Requires effective coordination, leadership, and collaboration.
5.Examples: Team meetings, classroom discussions, brainstorming sessions, group projects.
Aristotle's Model of Communication
What it is: A simple way to explain how one person (the speaker) talks to a group of people (the audience).
Main Points:
1.The speaker creates a message.
2.The speaker says the message using words or actions.
3.The audience listens and reacts.
4.Why it’s important: It shows how a good speaker can make people understand or believe something, like in speeches or ads.
Berlo's SMCR Model
What it is: A way to show how people send and receive messages. It focuses on four parts:
Main Points:
1.Source (S): The person who sends the message.
2.Message (M): What they want to say.
3.Channel (C): How the message is sent (like talking, writing, or showing pictures).
4.Receiver (R): The person who gets the message.
5.Why it’s important: It helps us understand that things like language, culture, and feelings can affect how messages are understood.
Shannon and Weaver's Model
What it is: A model that explains how messages travel, especially in technology.How it works:
Main Points:
1.Someone sends a message.
2.The message is turned into signals (like sound or images).
3.Sometimes, noise (like bad sound or distractions) can mess up the message.
4.The signals go through a channel (like the internet or a phone line).
5.The message is received and turned back into words or images.
6.Why it’s important: It shows how to send messages clearly and avoid problems like noise.
MODES OF COMMUNICATIONS
Modes of communication refer to the various ways in which people exchange information. These can be broadly classified into the following categories -
Verbal Communication
1.Oral Communication: Spoken words, such as conversations, speeches, and phone calls.
2.Written Communication: Written words, including emails, letters, reports, and text messages.
NON VERBAL COMMUNICATION
1.Body Language: Gestures, posture, facial expressions, and eye contact.
2.Paralanguage: Tone, pitch, volume, and speed of speaking.
3.Proxemics: The use of personal space and physical distance in communication.
4.Haptics: Communication through touch, like handshakes or hugs.
Visual Communication
1. Images and Graphics: Photographs, illustrations, charts, graphs, and infographics.
2. Signs and Symbols: Icons, logos, and symbols that convey specific meanings.
3. Visual Media: Television, films, advertisements, and online videos.
Digital Communication
1.Social Media: Platforms like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
2. Instant Messaging: Applications like WhatsApp, Slack, and Telegram.
3. Video Conferencing: Tools like Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Google Meet.
4. Email: Electronic mail communication.
Audio-Visual Communication
1.Multimedia Presentations: Combining text, images, audio, and video in presentations.
2. Webinars and Online Courses: Educational content delivered through video and audio formats.
Formal and Informal Communication
1.Formal Communication: Structured communication like official reports, memos, and business meetings.
2.Informal Communication: Casual or unstructured communication like gossip, informal chats, and social interactions.
